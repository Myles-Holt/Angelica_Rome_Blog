Angelica Rome Study Abroad

Studying abroad in Rome during the spring semester of 2026 gave me the chance to experience Italy in a way that was both exciting and personal. What started as a nervous first international trip quickly became a semester full of new routines, friendships, classes, and travel. I spent time learning the city through daily walks, public transportation, local markets, churches, museums, and neighborhoods like Trastevere and Pigneto. Some of the most meaningful moments came from connecting with my family’s history in Rome and seeing places that had always been part of family stories.

During the semester, I also traveled throughout Italy, visiting cities such as Bologna, Venice, Florence, Viterbo, Naples, Pompeii, Salerno, Paestum, and Milan. Each place showed me a different side of the country, from art and architecture to food, history, religion, and everyday life. Whether I was seeing famous landmarks, sharing meals with friends and family, celebrating Easter in a small town, or exploring Rome with my boyfriend, the semester helped me become more independent, adaptable, and connected to Italian culture.
